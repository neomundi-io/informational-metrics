# Dv - Volumetric Density

## Working English Version

This document presents a public and methodological definition of volumetric density as used within the NeoMundi framework.

It does not disclose proprietary parameters, production thresholds, full decision rules, or internal mechanisms used in the NeoMundi engine.

## Definition

Volumetric density refers to the observable relationship between the informational volume provided as input and the informational volume produced as output.

It makes it possible to characterize how an AI generation transforms, contracts, or amplifies the volume of information.

In its simplest conceptual form, it may be understood as a ratio between output volume and input volume.

## Role of Volumetric Density

Volumetric density makes it possible to observe whether a generation produces:

- a response shorter than the request;
- a response proportionate to the request;
- significant informational expansion;
- a potentially verbose output;
- a synthesis;
- an amplification of the initial content.

It does not indicate whether the response is true, stable, or useful.  
It describes a relationship of volume.

## Interpretation

Low volumetric density may correspond to a concise, synthetic, or insufficient response.

High volumetric density may correspond to a detailed, explanatory, or overly verbose response.

Volumetric density must therefore always be interpreted with the generation context, the task type, and the other available signals.

## What Dv Does Not Claim to Measure

Dv does not claim to measure:

- semantic truth;
- factual certainty;
- governance stability;
- the human quality of a response;
- the presence or absence of hallucination;
- actual usefulness for the user;
- the legal responsibility of a generation.

Dv measures a relationship of volume, not validity.

## Relationship with Volumetry

Volumetry measures the observed volumes.

Dv introduces a relational reading between these volumes.

Volumetry answers the question: how much information was handled?

Dv answers the question: in what proportion did the system transform input volume into output volume?

## Relationship with Other Metrics

Dv can help contextualize:

- informational energy;
- informational density;
- length effects;
- stability variations;
- governance decisions.

However, Dv should not be confused with G, ΔG, E, or Di.

Dv observes a volumetric transformation.  
G observes a stability state.  
ΔG observes a variation in stability.  
E and Di describe other informational dimensions.

## Methodological Position

Volumetric density is a descriptive metric.

It is intended to make visible the relationship between the volume requested and the volume produced.

It helps better understand generative behaviors, especially in cases of very long, very short, disproportionate, or highly synthetic responses.

## Limits

High volumetric density is not necessarily negative.

Low volumetric density is not necessarily positive.

Everything depends on the context:

- a legal response may require strong expansion;
- a synthesis may require strong contraction;
- a pedagogical response may be long but useful;
- a short response may hide insufficiency or ambiguity.

Dv must therefore be interpreted as an indicator of volumetric transformation, never as an isolated judgment of quality.
